# Sharpening the Social Top-of-Funnel

GoDaily already posts to several platforms on a schedule — a daily
featured story, a weekly recap, source spotlights, and explicit
sign-up calls to action. At the current stage this automated presence is
the single widest net the product casts. The opportunity is not to add
more channels, but to make the posts that already go out work harder.

## Why this exists

For a small audience, the daily social post is the most reliable way
strangers encounter GoDaily at all. That makes the *quality* of those
posts the highest-leverage variable in the whole funnel. A post that a
respected Go developer reshares reaches an audience GoDaily could never
buy; a post that reads as an advert gets scrolled past.

The instinct when growth is slow is to push harder — more "sign up!"
posts, more frequency. At this stage that backfires. The thing that
actually spreads is a genuinely useful post about a genuinely
interesting story, with the subscribe invitation kept soft and secondary.
Usefulness is what earns the reshare, and the reshare is what reaches new
people.

## The end result

- **The daily post stands on its own.** Someone who never subscribes
  still finds the post worth reading — which is exactly why they pass it
  along to someone who might.
- **Reshares do the recruiting.** The posts most worth spreading are the
  ones GoDaily leans on, because a reshare from a trusted account is the
  most credible introduction the product can get.
- **The ask is present but quiet.** Every post makes it easy to find and
  follow GoDaily, without leading with the ask or sounding like
  marketing.
- **One great story beats one more plea.** Effort shifts from producing
  more sign-up prompts to making each featured story as sharp and
  shareable as it can be.

## What success looks like

The daily featured post earns reshares and engagement from real members
of the Go community, not just impressions, and a meaningful share of new
subscribers can trace their first contact to social. The account reads
like a knowledgeable peer worth following — not a billboard.

## What this is not

This is not about posting more often or turning up the volume on the
call to action. It deliberately favours restraint: fewer, better, more
useful posts, consistent with a brand that does not hard-sell.
