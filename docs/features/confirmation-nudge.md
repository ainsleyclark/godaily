# The Confirmation Nudge

GoDaily uses a double opt-in: someone enters their email, and they only
join the daily send once they click the confirmation link. Inevitably,
some people sign up and never confirm — they get distracted, the email
slips down the inbox, the moment passes. Those people wanted in and never
made it. The confirmation nudge is a single, polite reminder that helps
them finish what they started.

## Why this exists

Unconfirmed sign-ups are the warmest audience GoDaily has and the easiest
to lose. They have already raised their hand; the only thing standing
between them and the digest is one unclicked link. Recovering even a
fraction of them is pure upside, and unlike most growth tactics it
requires no new audience — it rescues intent that already exists and is
quietly leaking away.

It is also the rare re-engagement message that is entirely on-brand. This
is not chasing someone who said no. It is helping someone who said yes
cross the finish line. Done once and done well, it reads as a courtesy,
not a pitch.

## The end result

- **Stalled sign-ups get a second chance.** People who registered but
  never confirmed receive one gentle reminder that their subscription
  isn't active yet, with an easy way to complete it.
- **Recovered intent becomes real subscribers.** A share of those who
  would otherwise have been lost convert into confirmed, engaged readers
  — the highest-quality kind, because they explicitly wanted in.
- **It stays a courtesy, not a campaign.** A single, well-timed,
  respectful reminder — not a drip sequence, not pressure. Someone who
  still doesn't confirm is left alone.
- **The funnel stops leaking silently.** A loss that currently happens
  invisibly becomes a recoverable step, and the gap between "signed up"
  and "actually subscribed" narrows.

## What success looks like

The rate at which sign-ups convert into confirmed subscribers rises,
because fewer of them fall through the gap between intent and action. The
reminder itself draws little to no complaint, because it is read as
helpful rather than pushy.

## What this is not

This is not re-engagement spam and it does not target people who
unsubscribed or asked to be left alone. It is a one-time, opt-in-completing
courtesy aimed solely at people who started the process and never
finished — fully consistent with the "stay classy" promise.
